Valentyna Karpets-Yermolaieva (born June 27, 1970, Mykolaivka, Verkhnodniprovsk district) is a master of Petrykivka painting and Honored Master of Folk Art of Ukraine (2007). She is a member of the National Union of Folk Art Masters of Ukraine (since 1991) and the National Union of Artists of Ukraine (since 1994).

## Education

From 1987 to 1989 she studied at the Petrykivka Vocational School, specializing in wood painting; in 1989–1990 she studied privately under [Olena Zinchuk](/en/masters/olena-zinchuk).

## Work

Since 1990 she has worked as a Petrykivka painting master — first at the [Experimental Workshop](/en/centers/eksperymentalnyy-tsekh) of the Dnipropetrovsk Art and Production Combine, and since then at the [Petrykivka Folk Art Center](/en/centers/tsentr-narodnoho-mystectva). She paints panels on paper and wood, decorative plates, vases, toys, interiors, and furniture; her favorite subjects are flowers and birds.

## Recognition

Laureate of the Maria Prymachenko Prize (2008) and the Taras Shevchenko National Prize of Ukraine (2025, jointly with [Natalia Rybak](/en/masters/nataliya-rybak), [Andriy](/en/masters/andriy-pikush), and [Maria Pikush](/en/masters/mariya-pikush)) for the project "Petrykivka Painting: Defending Identity."

## Collections

Her works are held in the [Dnipro Art Museum](/en/centers/dniprovskyi-khudozhnii-muzei), the [Zaporizhzhia Art Museum](/en/centers/zaporizkyi-khudozhnii-muzei), the [National Museum of Decorative Art](/en/centers/natsionalnyi-muzei-dekorativnoho-mystectva), and private collections in Canada, the US, and Japan.
