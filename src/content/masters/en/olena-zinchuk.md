Olena Zinchuk (born September 23, 1959, Petrykivka) is an artist of decorative painting and a member of the National Union of Artists of Ukraine (since 1987).

## Education

She graduated from the Dnipropetrovsk Art College in 1978, studying under [Andriy Pikush](/en/masters/andriy-pikush). Since 1994 she has lived in Montreal, Canada.

## Work

She has taken part in regional, all-Ukrainian (since 1983), and international (since 1985) exhibitions. She paints in gouache and tempera on wood, using the material's natural texture; her palette is built mainly around a red-orange range.

Major works: the panels "Bouquet" (1981), "Mallows," "Roosters" (1982), "Wreath," "Wild Rose" (1983), "Dahlias" (1984), "Blue Birds on an Apple Tree" (1985), "Bouquet of Yellow Flowers" (1987); the vase "Roses" and decanter "Pink" (both 1988); the barrel "Poppy" (1990); a pysanky series (1990–1994); the plate and chest "Wedding" (1984/1992, 1992).

Her student is [Valentyna Karpets-Yermolaieva](/en/masters/valentyna-karpets-yermolaieva).

## Collections

Her works are held in the [Dnipro Art Museum](/en/centers/dniprovskyi-khudozhnii-muzei) and the [Zaporizhzhia Art Museum](/en/centers/zaporizkyi-khudozhnii-muzei).
